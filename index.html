Observational Astrophysics · Planet Formation

Anuroop Dasgupta

I study how protoplanetary disks turn into planetary systems, and what observable signatures trace that change.

I am a PhD candidate at the European Southern Observatory and Universidad Diego Portales in Santiago, Chile. My work spans the spectrum from millimeter to optical wavelengths, using ALMA, VLT/ERIS, VLTI/GRAVITY, CHARA, and JWST. Alongside the science, I build the reduction and modeling pipelines that the analysis depends on.

What I work on

From the outer disk to the inner au

All research →
01 · OUTER DISK

Disk demographics

I measure disk sizes and structure with ALMA across young star-forming regions, and connect what I find to multiplicity, evolutionary stage, and how efficiently planets can form.

02 · COMPANIONS

Embedded companions

I use high-contrast imaging to find young, still-accreting companions inside eruptive systems, including a new companion candidate near V960 Mon that we reported in 2025.

03 · ERUPTIVE STARS

FU Orionis outbursts

I search time-domain surveys for FU Orionis outbursts and follow up on the disks behind them, from a systematic ZTF search to a combined JWST and ALMA view of the FUor HBC 687.

04 · INNER DISK

Sub-au structure

With optical and infrared interferometry on VLTI/GRAVITY and CHARA, I resolve the innermost disk: its geometry, temperature gradients, and how it gets cleared.
