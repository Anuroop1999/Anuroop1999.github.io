Research snapshot

I study how disks evolve from the outer dust reservoir to the inner AU, and how this evolution connects to planet formation and episodic accretion. I combine multi-wavelength observations with instrument-adaptable pipelines and statistical modeling.

  • Disk structure & evolution: ALMA size distributions, multiplicity, and evolutionary stage diagnostics.
  • Substellar companions in embedded systems: ERIS/vAPP high-contrast imaging + contrast-to-mass interpretation.
  • Inner disk morphology & kinematics: VLTI/GRAVITY + CHARA constraints on sub-AU structure.

Currently

ESO Studentship (2025–2027). Working on interferometric modeling and multi-technique follow-up of young systems with complex disk substructures.

Open to

Collaborations on eruptive YSOs, inner disk imaging, and combined ALMA+HCI+interferometry constraints.

Research

Organize by themes (like the example site) instead of a long paragraph.

1) Outer disks: demographics & evolution

ALMA continuum sizes, substructure statistics, and links to age/multiplicity/environment.

  • Size distribution measurements & uncertainty propagation
  • Comparisons across regions / evolutionary classes
  • Interpreting trends with drift / fragmentation / pressure traps

2) Eruptive stars & embedded companions

FUor outbursts, gravitational instability, and identifying deeply embedded substellar objects.

  • ERIS/vAPP reduction + ADI/RDI style workflows
  • Contrast curves → mass limits with model caveats
  • Multi-epoch astrometry strategy (comoving tests)

3) Inner disks: interferometric constraints

Sub-AU morphology, temperature gradients, and asymmetries using VLTI/CHARA observables.

  • Geometric & temperature-gradient models
  • Closure phase / differential phase interpretation
  • Binary-driven clearing / inner rim structure

4) Mid-IR mineralogy (planned/ongoing)

Crystalline silicates and thermal processing during outbursts; the 10 μm feature as a tracer.

  • VISIR spectra + telluric correction strategy
  • Feature decomposition & crystallinity metrics
  • Connecting outburst physics to dust processing
