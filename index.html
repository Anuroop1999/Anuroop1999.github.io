Observational Astrophysics · Planet Formation

Anuroop Dasgupta

I reconstruct how protoplanetary disks evolve into planetary systems — from their outer dust reservoirs down to sub-au inner regions.

PhD candidate at the European Southern Observatory (ESO) and Universidad Diego Portales, Santiago, Chile. I work across the electromagnetic spectrum — ALMA, VLT/ERIS, VLTI/GRAVITY, CHARA, and JWST — and build the reduction and modeling pipelines this work depends on.

What I work on

An end-to-end view of planet formation

All research →
01 / OUTER DISK

Disk demographics

ALMA continuum sizes and structure across young regions, linking morphology to multiplicity, evolutionary stage, and planet-formation efficiency (ODISEA).

02 / COMPANIONS

Embedded companions

High-contrast imaging of accreting substellar objects in eruptive systems — including a new companion candidate near V960 Mon (ApJL 2025).

03 / INNER DISK

Sub-au structure

Optical/IR interferometry with VLTI/GRAVITY and CHARA to resolve inner-disk geometry, temperature gradients, and clearing mechanisms.
