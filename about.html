About me

A bit about me

I am an observational astrophysicist and PhD candidate based at the European Southern Observatory and Universidad Diego Portales in Santiago, Chile. My research is about how protoplanetary disks, the disks of gas and dust around young stars, evolve into planetary systems, and what observable signatures trace that transformation.

To follow that story I work across the spectrum, from millimeter wavelengths with ALMA out to the optical and infrared with VLT/ERIS, VLTI/GRAVITY, CHARA, and JWST. The questions range from how big disks are and how they are structured, to the eruptive young stars and embedded companions caught in the act of forming, down to the inner au resolved with interferometry. A large part of my work is building the reduction and modeling pipelines that make this analysis uniform and reproducible across very different instruments.

I grew up and studied physics in Kolkata, India, before moving to Chile for my doctorate, drawn by its access to the world's leading observatories. Alongside research, I care about making astronomy reachable, through public observing, outreach with school communities, and science communication across India and Chile.
