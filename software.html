Pipelines built for the data

Most of my science depends on turning raw observations into clean, reproducible measurements, and that means building the pipeline as carefully as designing the observation. These are the four end-to-end pipelines behind my current projects, written in Python and built to stay reproducible across very different instruments. Repositories are released alongside the papers they belong to.

GitHub
Time-domain · ZTF / ALeRCE

FU Orionis discovery pipeline

An end-to-end search that mines the full ZTF archive, around 131 million sources served through the ALeRCE broker, for FU Orionis accretion outbursts. A multi-stage selection cascade runs detection and baseline cuts, WISE infrared colour selection of disk-bearing young stars, amplitude and light-curve morphology criteria, and Lomb–Scargle vetting. It recovers all known ZTF-era FUors, including Gaia18dvy and Gaia20bdk, and yields 245 vetted candidates, the largest uniformly selected FUor sample to date. A real-time weekly monitor issues automated alerts for target-of-opportunity follow-up and is designed to carry over to the Rubin/LSST alert stream.

Interferometry · VLTI / CHARA

Temperature-gradient disk model

A physically motivated disk model that jointly fits spectrally dispersed near-infrared squared visibilities and the broadband spectral energy distribution. It computes disk visibilities through the Hankel transform of a radial temperature profile, and combines Nelder–Mead optimisation with emcee MCMC for parameter estimation, with optional halo and compact-component extensions. I use it to constrain inner-rim temperatures, radii, and temperature gradients across the Herbig Ae/Be survey.

Python emcee MCMC Hankel transform SED + visibilities
High-contrast imaging · VLT / ERIS

ERIS L′-band imaging pipeline

A dedicated angular differential imaging and PSF-subtraction pipeline with precision astrometric extraction for VLT/ERIS. Applied to V960 Mon, it yielded the detection of a new substellar companion candidate, the result behind my 2025 ApJL paper and the accompanying ESO press release.

Spectroscopy · JWST / MIRI MRS

JWST MIRI MRS reduction & analysis

A custom reduction and analysis layer built on the MINDS, jwst, and VIP framework for MIRI MRS data of FUor stars, covering 5 to 28 microns. It resolves IFU-cube recentering failures through peak-finding on median-collapsed cubes, and adds extinction correction, multi-blackbody and spline continuum fitting, and dust, ice, and outflow diagnostics including the 10 micron silicate feature, CO2 ice, [Ne II], and H2.

Tools and methods

Languages

Python as the primary language, with Fortran, Mathematica, Bash, and LaTeX, working with Git and a test-driven approach.

Astronomy stack

CASA, PMOIRED, the jwst / MINDS / VIP framework, Astropy, Specutils, emcee, IRAF, DS9, TOPCAT, and Streamlit.

Analysis

Interferometric visibility modeling, Bayesian inference with MCMC, Lomb–Scargle periodograms, multi-wavelength photometry, spectral line fitting, and bootstrap and Monte Carlo error analysis.

Pipelines and data

End-to-end, instrument-adaptable workflows, large-scale database queries with SQL and the ALeRCE API, and automated real-time alert systems.
