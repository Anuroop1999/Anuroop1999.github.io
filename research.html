Research

I work across outer-to-inner disk scales, connecting demographics (ALMA) to embedded companions (HCI) and sub-au structure (interferometry).

Outer disks (ALMA/ODISEA)

Using high-frequency ALMA continuum imaging, I study disk size distributions and their dependence on evolutionary class, multiplicity, and environment. The goal is to connect observed morphology to dust evolution (drift, growth, traps) and disk clearing pathways.

  • Uniform size measurements across large samples
  • Demographics vs multiplicity and evolutionary stage
  • Links to dust evolution/planet formation models

Eruptive stars & embedded companions (ERIS)

I use L′-band high-contrast imaging to search for deeply embedded companions in FU Ori-type systems, where gravitational instability may trigger fragmentation. Multi-wavelength context (e.g., ALMA clumps, scattered-light structures) is used to interpret candidate detections.

  • Second-epoch astrometry to test co-motion
  • Photometric calibration + contrast curves
  • Connecting clumps/spirals to fragmentation scenarios

Inner disks (VLTI/GRAVITY & CHARA)

I analyze optical/IR interferometric data to constrain inner-disk geometry, asymmetries, temperature gradients, and the role of close companions in clearing inner cavities and shaping disk morphology.

  • Temperature-gradient & multi-component geometric models
  • Closure-phase signatures of asymmetry/binarity
  • Statistical study of Herbig inner disk structures

Methods & pipelines

High-contrast imaging

ADI/RDI workflows, PSF subtraction, injection-recovery, contrast-to-mass conversions, astrometry.

Interferometry

Visibility/closure-phase modeling, multi-component fits, photometric consistency checks, uncertainty propagation.

Millimeter imaging

Uniform measurement pipelines for sizes, fluxes, beam effects, and sample-level statistics.

Reproducibility

Instrument-adaptable pipelines, structured outputs, and versioned analysis notebooks/scripts.
